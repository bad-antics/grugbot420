## What is grugbot420?

grugbot420 is a **specimen-based cognitive runtime** built around distributed nodes, quorum behavior, domain partitioning, upkeep, and persistence. It models cognition through competing populations of pattern nodes — not transformers, not lookup tables, not if-else waterfalls. Many rocks compete to be loudest. Loudest rock gets to talk. Sometimes a quiet rock gets lucky (coinflip).

## Core Concepts

- **Nodes** — The atomic unit of cognition. Each has a pattern, action packet, strength, and topology.
- **Lobes** — Domain partitions that group related nodes and reduce cross-domain noise.
- **Specimens** — Persistent snapshots of the entire cognitive state. Save, share, restore.
- **Quorum Voting** — Output is resolved through weighted competition, not a single deterministic path.
- **Stochastic Coinflip** — Randomness is a feature. Weak nodes can get lucky. Strong nodes can lose.

## Philosophy

GrugBot was not born from textbooks. It emerged from direct contact with signal, structure, failure, and recurrence. Most modern systems start from an abstract brain and bolt on helpers afterward. GrugBot begins with **organs, gates, thresholds, partitions, upkeep, and field behavior**. Cognition is not assumed at the center — it is permitted to emerge from aligned subsystems.
